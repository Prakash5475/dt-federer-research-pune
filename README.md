# DT CultureTech Round 1 Submission

## Selected City

Pune

## Selected Segments

1. Industrial Sensors & Instrumentation
2. Precision Auto Components & Engineering

## Research Objective

Identify Federer-profile manufacturing companies that align with DeepThought's execution consulting model and PDGMS platform opportunity.

## Research Process

1. Built an initial candidate universe using company websites, LinkedIn, industry directories, MIDC clusters, manufacturing associations, and publicly available business information.
2. Applied **E1 – Producer** screening to confirm manufacturing capability or proprietary specialized services.
3. Applied **E2 – Accessibility** screening to confirm India-based operational presence and identifiable leadership.
4. Excluded traders, distributors, resellers, MNC/JV-controlled entities, and companies outside the preferred revenue band.
5. Conducted manual verification of ownership structure, product portfolio, operational presence, and leadership information.
6. Evaluated shortlisted companies using the **C3–C8 Federer scoring framework**.
7. Assigned confidence ratings based on the strength and quality of publicly available evidence.

## Deliverables

- Final Company List (30 companies)
- Fail List (49 companies)
- Methodology Document
- Research Notes and Evidence

## Key Observation

Pune hosts a concentrated cluster of precision engineering, industrial automation, instrumentation, and component manufacturing firms. This density enabled efficient sourcing of founder-led and operationally accessible companies that closely match the Federer profile.

## Candidate Universe

- Initial Universe: 79 companies
- Final Shortlist: 30 companies
- Rejected: 49 companies
- Shortlisting Rate: 38%

## Screening Gates

### E1 – Producer

The company must manufacture products or deliver proprietary specialized services rather than operate primarily as a trader, distributor, reseller, or representative organization.

### E2 – Accessible

The company must have a clear operational presence in India and identifiable leadership or decision-makers that can reasonably be reached through public channels.

### Revenue Band Suitability

Preference given to companies operating within the Federer profile range, avoiding very small firms with limited operational scale and large enterprises exceeding the target segment.

### Ownership Structure

Preference given to founder-led, privately controlled, or independently operated businesses. MNC-controlled entities and joint ventures were excluded.

### Evidence Availability

Sufficient publicly available information had to exist to support scoring across the Federer framework. Companies lacking verifiable evidence were excluded.

## Federer Scoring Framework

### C3 – Differentiation

Assessment of unique products, engineering capability, specialization, certifications, market positioning, or defensible expertise.

### C4 – Decision-Maker Quality

Assessment of leadership visibility, founder involvement, management quality, and organizational accessibility.

### C5 – Growing Sector

Evaluation of whether the company operates in sectors with favorable long-term industrial growth trends.

### C6 – Growth Signals

Evidence such as hiring activity, facility expansion, new product launches, export presence, certifications, industry participation, or recent business development initiatives.

### C7 – Systems Maturity

Assessment of operational sophistication, manufacturing processes, quality systems, certifications, digitalization, and organizational structure.

### C8 – Leadership Succession

Evaluation of continuity indicators, second-generation leadership involvement, professional management development, or long-term organizational sustainability.
